DCOIR Structure And Refactor Lane

1. Review large governed logs and continuity files for sustainable split candidates.
   Why it matters:
   - Growth-prone files should be restructured before they become brittle.

2. Normalize naming and folder conventions where older experimental paths remain.
   Why it matters:
   - Consistent naming reduces confusion and eases future updates.

3. Review any remaining legacy or orphaned split-attempt artifacts before cleanup.
   Why it matters:
   - Old partial structures should not be removed until dependency checks confirm nothing still relies on them.

4. Preserve cross-file traceability whenever governed files are split.
   Why it matters:
   - Resume and governance must remain clean even after structural refactors.

5. Review whether any remaining repo delivery artifacts still lag the current split todo and task-memory posture.
   Why it matters:
   - Packaging and delivery artifacts should not quietly drift behind the live control plane.

6. Audit the repo for true duplicate content and remove only verified duplicates.
   Why it matters:
   - Duplicate readable material, duplicate extracted sources, or duplicated supporting assets can create confusion, cleanup risk, and maintenance drift if the repo only checks names or paths.
   Next action:
   - Compare actual content rather than only filenames or locations, remove only verified duplicates, and if connector-only comparison proves too cumbersome, define the easiest automation-first operator path with simple step-by-step instructions and minimal manual work.
