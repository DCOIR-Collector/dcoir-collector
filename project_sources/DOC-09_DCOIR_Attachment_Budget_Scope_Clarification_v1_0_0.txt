DCOIR Attachment-Budget Scope Clarification v1.0.0

PURPOSE
- Prevent future confusion between collector-emitted analyst-facing artifacts and the project’s backbone deliverables.
- Preserve the full substance of governed source and runtime deliverables while still enforcing realistic Gemini upload limits for actual analyst-facing attachment sets.

SCOPE RULE
- The office Gemini upload-budget rule applies only to:
  - collector-emitted analyst-facing artifacts
  - default Gemini attachment sets built from those artifacts
  - other explicitly operator-facing attachment bundles whose purpose is direct prompt upload

BACKBONE DELIVERABLES EXCLUDED FROM ATTACHMENT BUDGETING
- The upload-budget rule does not require shrinking, chunking, or thinning the backbone deliverables.
- Backbone deliverables include at minimum:
  - project_sources/DCOIR_Collector.ps1
  - project_sources/collector_parts/*.ps1
  - project_sources/run_DCOIR_Tests.ps1
  - project_sources/PP-01 through project_sources/PP-10
  - the full standalone master prompt assembly
  - Gemini parent-agent and sub-agent definition markdowns
  - other governed source bundles whose job is to preserve full project behavior rather than serve as prompt-upload artifacts

PACKAGING RULE
- If email, transport, or platform constraints affect a backbone deliverable, solve the transport problem separately.
- Mail-safe suffixing, wrapper files, or delivery reshaping are acceptable when they preserve the full governed substance.
- Do not silently reduce, condense, or fragment a backbone deliverable just to satisfy attachment-size targets unless the operator explicitly directs that tradeoff.

VALIDATION RULE
- Bundle verification and QC must distinguish between:
  - budget-scoped attachment files
  - backbone deliverables
- It is a validation failure to apply collector-artifact attachment ceilings to a backbone deliverable and then present the reduced result as if it were the intended governed bundle.

END STATE
- The project should preserve full-strength collector, prompt-pack, standalone, and Gemini-definition deliverables while separately enforcing realistic upload limits for the actual analyst-facing artifacts that must be attached to Gemini prompts.
